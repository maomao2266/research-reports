The bull case (€846, +24%) requires either (a) CMD delivering a bullish 2030 target, or (b) market re-rating ASML as a compounder rather than cyclical. Both are credible catalysts (see Step 7).

Pitfalls Acknowledged

  1. DCF is one input: Per Hooke (2010), Wall Street weights DCF at only ~20% in final valuation decisions. Comps, precedent transactions, and market-implied growth are equally important.
  2. Forecast fragility: Even 1-year EBIT forecasts have 25-30% mean error. 5-year projections are inherently uncertain.
  3. Terminal value dominance: 80% of EV lives in terminal value. A 0.5% change in terminal growth shifts per-share value by ~€40.
  4. Tax rate uncertainty: ASML's IP Box regime is under EU review. A shift from 18% to 25% effective tax rate would reduce per-share value by ~15%.
  5. Customer concentration risk: €608 base case assumes TSMC/Intel/Samsung continue ordering. Any single customer capex cut creates a 5-10% earnings hole not captured in the smooth projection.
